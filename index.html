Interactive Experience Analysis

Frank Gehry – Walt Disney Concert Hall

Visit The Website

First Impression

Question 1: What was the first thing you paid attention to when interacting with the experience?

The first thing I noticed was the classical music playing in the background and the cinematic animations. The moving 3D model of the Walt Disney Concert Hall immediately stood out, and the smooth scrolling transitions made the website feel very grand and immersive, almost like entering a digital exhibition.

User Interaction

Question 2: Spend two minutes with the experience and create a list of each of your discrete actions.

  • Opened the homepage of the interactive experience
  • Noticed the background music starting when I clicked on the webpage just after entering it
  • The continuous scrolling down to unlock more information, it was one long webpage
  • Watched the animated 3D model rotate
  • Continued scrolling to the next chapter section
  • Hovered over or interacted with a 3D model to rotate or explore it
  • Clicked or explored additional content related to the design process
  • Scrolled through images and sketches of the building models
  • Clicked on the videos to make it start playing
  • Paused briefly to read text explaining the design process

Question 3: What part of the experience did you spend the most time engaging with?

I spent the most time interacting with the 3D models and visual animations of the building design. Rotating and examining the models was the most engaging part because it allowed me to explore the architecture in a more interactive way.

Question 4: What was the most common action in your two minute interaction with the experience?

The most common action was scrolling. The experience is largely scroll driven, with new sections, images, and videos appearing as you move down the page.

Purpose

Question 5: What is your impression of the intended primary goal of the interactive experience?

The main goal seems to be educating visitors about how the Walt Disney Concert Hall was designed by Frank Gehry while showcasing the creative process behind the architecture.

Question 6: How does the interactive experience communicate this primary goal?

The experience communicates this goal through storytelling, visuals, and interactive elements such as the animated 3D models , archival images, and videos. These elements guide users through the stages of the building’s design process and make the information more engaging.

Interaction Over Time

Question 7:What is your impression of how the experience should be interacted with over time? (For how long and how many different times)

It feels like the experience is designed to be explored slowly in a single session, similar to walking through a digital museum exhibition. However, it could also be revisited multiple times if someone wants to explore specific sections more deeply.

Question 8: How does the interactive experience communicate how it should be interacted with over time?

The site encourages slow exploration through its scrolling narrative structure, large visuals, and sections that reveal information gradually. The pacing of animations and music also makes it feel like something to experience rather than quickly skim.

Media References

Question 9: What other media forms (digital or otherwise) does the experience reference?

The experience references several media forms:

  • Museum Exhibition
  • Documentary storytelling
  • Architectural models
  • Classical music performances
  • Interactive 3D digital visualizations

Question 10: What does this reference/s communicate to you about how you should act when engaging with your research experience?

These references suggest that the user should explore the experience thoughtfully and take time to observe the visuals and read the information, similar to how someone would interact with a museum exhibit.

Question 11:What does this reference/s communicate to you about how you should feel when engaging with your research experience?

The references create a sense of curiosity and appreciation for architecture and design. The music and visuals make the experience feel impressive and inspiring, emphasizing the creativity and complexity involved in designing the concert hall.

Evaluation

Question 12: What is the most frustrating part of the interaction to you and what makes that part frustrating?

The most frustrating part is that some interactions rely heavily on scrolling and animations, which can make it slower to navigate or difficult to quickly find specific information. They force you to walk through all the information like a museum exhibition, so it doesn’t allow you to skip through quickly to find specific details.

Question 13: What is the most satisfying part of the interaction to you and what makes that part satisfying?

The most satisfying part is interacting with the 3D models and watching the animations. Being able to explore the building’s design visually makes the experience feel immersive and helps communicate the architectural concepts more clearly
